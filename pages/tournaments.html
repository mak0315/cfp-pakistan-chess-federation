Pakistan National Chess Championship

The Pakistan National Chess Championship is the premier domestic chess event, organized annually by CFP. The championship determines the national champion who represents Pakistan in international tournaments and qualifies for FIDE title norms. IM Mahmood Lodhi holds the all-time record with 16 national titles.

♟ Pakistan at the Chess Olympiad

Pakistan has participated in over 12 Chess Olympiads, with FM Amer Karim alone having represented Pakistan 6 times. Pakistan's finest Olympiad performance was in 2006 Turin where FM Amer Karim scored 7.5/8 on Board 5 with a performance rating of 2486, winning the Silver Medal on board performance.

2024

45th Chess Olympiad — Budapest, Hungary

Pakistan's delegation participated in both Open and Women's sections. Led by FM Amer Karim in his 6th Olympiad. Pakistan continued its tradition of international chess representation at the world's largest chess team event.

Open Team Participated
2022

44th Chess Olympiad — Chennai, India

Pakistan team participated in the historic Chennai Olympiad, the first time India hosted the event. Strong performances from the Pakistani contingent representing all five regions.

Open Team Participated
2006

37th Chess Olympiad — Turin, Italy ⭐ Best Result

Pakistan's historic Olympiad. FM Amer Karim scored 7.5/8 points on Board 5 with a remarkable performance rating of 2486, earning Pakistan the Silver Medal for Board 5 performance. This remains Pakistan's finest individual Olympiad achievement.

FM Amer Karim represented Pakistan in his second Olympiad appearance in Dresden. Pakistan's continued participation in FIDE's flagship team event.
