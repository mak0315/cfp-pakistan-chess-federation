About CFP

The Chess Federation of Pakistan — established 1957, FIDE-affiliated since 1973. Over six decades of growing chess in Pakistan.

Our Purpose

Mission & Vision

CFP is committed to promoting chess as a tool for intellectual development, cultural exchange, and national pride across all of Pakistan.

🌱

Grassroots Development

Establishing chess clubs, school programs, and training centers across all seven provinces to ensure every Pakistani child has access to the game.

🏆

Elite Performance

Supporting talented players to achieve FIDE titles, participate in international events, and represent Pakistan with distinction at the Chess Olympiad and World Championships.

📚

Chess in Education

Implementing FIDE's Chess in Education initiative in Pakistani schools, fostering critical thinking, patience, and strategic reasoning among students.

♀️

Women's Chess

Actively promoting women's participation in chess through dedicated tournaments, coaching programs, and pathways to earning FIDE titles.

🤝

International Relations

Strengthening Pakistan's position in FIDE, the Asian Chess Federation, and the Commonwealth Chess Association through active participation and leadership.

📊

Rating Development

Organizing FIDE-rated tournaments to help Pakistani players earn official ratings and titles, growing the country's rated player base year on year.

Our Journey

History of CFP

From independence to international recognition — the story of chess in Pakistan.

1957
Chess Federation of Pakistan Founded
The Chess Federation of Pakistan was established as the national governing body for chess, shortly after the country's independence. The federation began the task of organizing chess across the newly formed nation.
1973
FIDE Membership
Pakistan officially joined FIDE (World Chess Federation), opening the door for Pakistani players to participate in international tournaments, earn FIDE ratings, and compete at the Chess Olympiad.
1987
First International Master — Mahmood Lodhi
Mahmood Lodhi became Pakistan's first and only International Master, awarded the IM title by FIDE. He has one Grandmaster norm and went on to win the national championship a record 16 times.
2006
Historic Olympiad Silver — Turin
FM Amer Karim achieved Pakistan's best-ever individual Olympiad result: 7.5/8 on Board 5 with a performance rating of 2486 at the 37th Chess Olympiad in Turin, Italy, winning the Silver Medal.
2022
New CFP Leadership — Hanif Qureshi Elected President
Mr. Hanif Qureshi was unanimously elected President of CFP in June 2022. The National Championship was held in Gilgit-Baltistan — a landmark for chess in the northern territories. FM Amer Karim won the title.
2023–24
FIDE Chess for Freedom — Pakistan
A high-level FIDE delegation including President Arkady Dvorkovich, GM Nigel Short, and Asian Chess Federation President visited Pakistan. Pakistan's PM pledged to bring chess to every school, supported by FIDE's Chess for Freedom initiative.
2025
Record Youth Participation
The 3rd National Youth Chess Championship in Karachi drew over 250 young players — the largest youth chess event in Pakistan's history — marking a new era for the game's development.
