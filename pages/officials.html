Meet the elected leadership and executive committee steering chess development across Pakistan.

Executive Committee

Elected in June 2022, the current CFP executive committee is committed to developing chess at all levels across Pakistan.

👑
Hanif Qureshi
President, Chess Federation of Pakistan

Hanif Qureshi was unanimously elected President of the Chess Federation of Pakistan in June 2022. As chief patron and advocate for chess in Pakistan, he has championed the FIDE Chess for Freedom initiative and the expansion of chess into schools nationwide. He attended the historic meeting with FIDE President Arkady Dvorkovich and the Prime Minister of Pakistan to commit to chess education.

Member Organizations

Provincial Chess Associations

Seven provincial and regional chess associations are affiliated with CFP, covering all of Pakistan.

Punjab Chess Association

Largest provincial association covering Punjab — including Lahore, Rawalpindi, Faisalabad, and all major cities. Organizes inter-district and provincial championships.

Sindh Chess Association

Governing body for chess in Sindh province. Based in Karachi — the most active chess hub in Pakistan, hosting numerous FIDE-rated events.

Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Chess Association

Oversees chess development in KPK province, including Peshawar, Abbottabad, and surrounding areas. Rapidly growing grassroots chess base.

Balochistan Chess Association

Represents chess in Pakistan's largest province by area. Works to develop chess in Quetta and smaller cities throughout Balochistan.

Islamabad Chess Association

Governs chess in the Federal Capital Territory. Close collaboration with government bodies and diplomatic community in Islamabad.

Azad Kashmir Chess Association

Promotes and governs chess across Azad Jammu & Kashmir. Growing participation in national youth championships.

Gilgit-Baltistan Chess Association

Governing body for chess in Gilgit-Baltistan. Site of the 2022 National Chess Championship, marking the region's growing prominence.
